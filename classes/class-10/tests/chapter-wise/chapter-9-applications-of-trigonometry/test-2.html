Section B (2 Marks each)

Q5. An observer 1.5 m tall is 28.5 m away from a chimney. The angle of elevation of the top of the chimney from her eyes is 45°. What is the height of the chimney?

Solve on white paper.

Q6. The tops of two poles of height 20 m and 14 m are connected by a wire. If the wire makes an angle of 30° with the horizontal, what is the length of the wire?

Solve on white paper.

Q7. From a point on a bridge across a river, the angles of depression of the banks on opposite sides of the river are 30° and 45° respectively. If the bridge is at a height of 3 m from the banks, find the width of the river.

Section C (3 Marks each)

Q8. The angle of elevation of the top of a building from the foot of a tower is 30° and the angle of elevation of the top of the tower from the foot of the building is 60°. If the tower is 50 m high, find the height of the building.

Solve on white paper.

Q9. As observed from the top of a 75 m high lighthouse from the sea-level, the angles of depression of two ships are 30° and 45°. If one ship is exactly behind the other on the same side of the lighthouse, find the distance between the two ships.

Section D (5 Marks)

Q10. A 1.2 m tall girl spots a balloon moving with the wind in a horizontal line at a height of 88.2 m from the ground. The angle of elevation of the balloon from the eyes of the girl at any instant is 60°. After some time, the angle of elevation reduces to 30°. Find the distance travelled by the balloon during the interval.

Solve on white paper.

Section E (4 Marks)

Q11. Case Study:

A group of students of class X visited a lighthouse at the request of their teacher. The teacher said that the lighthouse is a tall tower with a bright light at the top, and it is used to guide ships and warn them of dangerous areas. From the top of the 100 m high lighthouse, they observe two ships on opposite sides of the lighthouse. The angles of depression to the ships are 30° and 45°.

(i) Draw a labelled figure on the basis of the given information.

(ii) Find the distance of the ship at 45° depression from the foot of the lighthouse.

(iii) Find the distance of the ship at 30° depression from the foot of the lighthouse.

(iv) What is the distance between the two ships?

Solve on white paper.
