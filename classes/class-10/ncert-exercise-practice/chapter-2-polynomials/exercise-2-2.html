Question 1

Find the zeroes of the following quadratic polynomials and verify the relationship between the zeroes and the coefficients.

(i) x² − 5x + 6

Step 1: Given polynomial: x² − 5x + 6

Step 2: Factorise:

x² − 5x + 6 = (x − 2)(x − 3)

Zeroes: 2 and 3

Verification:

Sum = 2 + 3 = 5 = −(−5)/1 ✔

Product = 2 × 3 = 6 = 6/1 ✔

(ii) 9x² − 12x + 4

Factorise:

9x² − 12x + 4 = (3x − 2)²

Zeroes: 2/3, 2/3

Sum = 4/3 = −(−12)/9 ✔

Product = 4/9 ✔

(iii) 6x² − 5x − 6

Factorise:

6x² − 5x − 6 = (3x + 2)(2x − 3)

Zeroes = −2/3 , 3/2

Sum = (−2/3 + 3/2) = 5/6 ✔

Product = −1 ✔

(iv) 4x² + 12x

Factorise:

4x(x + 3)

Zeroes = 0, −3

Sum = −3 ✔

Product = 0 ✔

(v) t² − 20

Factorise:

(t − √20)(t + √20)

Zeroes = √20 , −√20

Sum = 0 ✔

Product = −20 ✔

(vi) 3x² − 5x − 2

Factorise:

(3x + 1)(x − 2)

Zeroes = −1/3 , 2

Sum = 5/3 ✔

Product = −2/3 ✔

Question 2

Find a quadratic polynomial whose sum and product of zeroes are given.

(i) Sum = 1/2 , Product = −2

General form: x² − (sum)x + product

Polynomial = x² − (1/2)x − 2

Multiplying by 2:

2x² − x − 4

(ii) Sum = √3 , Product = 1/4

Polynomial = x² − √3 x + 1/4

(iii) Sum = 0 , Product = 6

Polynomial = x² + 6

(iv) Sum = 2 , Product = 1

Polynomial = x² − 2x + 1

(v) Sum = −1/2 , Product = 1/3

Polynomial = x² + (1/2)x + 1/3

(vi) Sum = 5 , Product = 6

Polynomial = x² − 5x + 6
