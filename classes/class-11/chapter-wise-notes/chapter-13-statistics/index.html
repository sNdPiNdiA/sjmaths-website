1. Measures of Dispersion

Dispersion measures how "spread out" the data is. The mean tells us the center, but dispersion tells us about the variation.

  • Range: Max Value - Min Value.
  • Mean Deviation: Average of absolute differences from Mean/Median.
  • Variance (\( \sigma^2 \)): Average of squared differences from Mean.
  • Standard Deviation (\( \sigma \)): Square root of Variance.

2. Formula Matrix

Measure Formula
Mean Deviation (\(\bar{x}\)) \( \frac{\sum |x_i - \bar{x}|}{n} \)
Mean Deviation (M) \( \frac{\sum |x_i - M|}{n} \)
Variance (\(\sigma^2\)) \( \frac{\sum (x_i - \bar{x})^2}{n} \)
Measure Formula
Mean Deviation (\(\bar{x}\)) \( \frac{\sum f_i |x_i - \bar{x}|}{N} \)
Variance (\(\sigma^2\)) \( \frac{1}{N^2}[N \sum f_i x_i^2 - (\sum f_i x_i)^2] \)
Standard Deviation \( \sqrt{\text{Variance}} \)

ðŸ§® Step-wise Method: Standard Deviation

Steps for Ungrouped Data:

  1. Find mean \( \bar{x} \)
  2. Find deviations \( x_i - \bar{x} \)
  3. Square deviations
  4. Find average of squared deviations
  5. Take square root

Formula:

\[ \sigma = \sqrt{\frac{\sum (x_i - \bar{x})^2}{n}} \]

3. Analysis of Frequency Distributions

To compare the variability of two series with different units, we use the Coefficient of Variation (C.V.).

\( C.V. = \frac{\sigma}{\bar{x}} \times 100 \)

Rule: Lower C.V. = More Consistent.
