Number Systems

Exercise 1.1 • Multiple Choice Questions (MCQs)

Q1

Every rational number is:

(A) A natural number
(B) An integer
(C) A real number
(D) A whole number

Correct Answer: (C) A real number

Explanation:

Real numbers are the set of all rational and irrational numbers. Since every rational number belongs to the set of real numbers, option (C) is correct.

Counter-examples for others:

  • $\frac{2}{3}$ is rational but not a natural number, integer, or whole number.
Q2

Between two rational numbers:

(A) There is no rational number
(B) There is exactly one rational number
(C) There are infinitely many rational numbers
(D) There are only rational numbers and no irrational numbers

Correct Answer: (C) There are infinitely many rational numbers

Explanation:

Between any two distinct rational numbers, there exist infinitely many rational numbers (and infinitely many irrational numbers as well). This property is known as the density property of rational numbers.

Q3

Decimal representation of a rational number cannot be:

(A) Terminating
(B) Non-terminating
(C) Non-terminating repeating
(D) Non-terminating non-repeating

Correct Answer: (D) Non-terminating non-repeating

Explanation:

A rational number $\frac{p}{q}$ has a decimal expansion that is either terminating or non-terminating repeating (recurring).

Non-terminating non-repeating decimal expansions correspond to irrational numbers.

Q4

The product of any two irrational numbers is:

(A) Always an irrational number
(B) Always a rational number
(C) Always an integer
(D) Sometimes rational, sometimes irrational

Correct Answer: (D) Sometimes rational, sometimes irrational

Explanation:

The product of two irrational numbers is not always irrational.

  • Example 1: $\sqrt{2} \times \sqrt{2} = 2$ (Rational)
  • Example 2: $\sqrt{2} \times \sqrt{3} = \sqrt{6}$ (Irrational)
Q5

The decimal expansion of the number $\sqrt{2}$ is:

(A) A finite decimal
(B) 1.41421
(C) Non-terminating recurring
(D) Non-terminating non-recurring

Correct Answer: (D) Non-terminating non-recurring

Explanation:

$\sqrt{2}$ is an irrational number. The decimal expansion of an irrational number is always non-terminating and non-recurring (non-repeating).

Q6

Which of the following is irrational?

(A) $\sqrt{\frac{4}{9}}$
(B) $\frac{\sqrt{12}}{\sqrt{3}}$
(C) $\sqrt{7}$
(D) $\sqrt{81}$

Correct Answer: (C) $\sqrt{7}$

Explanation:

  • (A) $\sqrt{\frac{4}{9}} = \frac{2}{3}$ (Rational)
  • (B) $\frac{\sqrt{12}}{\sqrt{3}} = \sqrt{4} = 2$ (Rational)
  • (C) $\sqrt{7}$ is not a perfect square, so it is irrational.
  • (D) $\sqrt{81} = 9$ (Rational)
Q7

Which of the following is rational?

(A) $\sqrt{3}$
(B) $\pi$
(C) $\frac{4}{0}$
(D) $\frac{0}{4}$

Correct Answer: (D) $\frac{0}{4}$

Explanation:

  • $\sqrt{3}$ is irrational.
  • $\pi$ is irrational.
  • $\frac{4}{0}$ is undefined.
  • $\frac{0}{4} = 0$, which is a rational number.
Q8

A rational number between $\sqrt{2}$ and $\sqrt{3}$ is:

(A) $\frac{\sqrt{2}+\sqrt{3}}{2}$
(B) $\frac{\sqrt{2}\cdot\sqrt{3}}{2}$
(C) 1.5
(D) 1.8

Correct Answer: (C) 1.5

Explanation:

We know that $\sqrt{2} \approx 1.414$ and $\sqrt{3} \approx 1.732$.

We need a rational number between 1.414 and 1.732.

1.5 lies between them and is rational ($1.5 = \frac{3}{2}$).

Q9

The value of $1.999...$ in the form $\frac{p}{q}$, where $p$ and $q$ are integers and $q \neq 0$, is:

(A) $\frac{19}{10}$
(B) $\frac{1999}{1000}$
(C) 2
(D) $\frac{1}{9}$

Correct Answer: (C) 2

Explanation:

Let $x = 1.999...$

$10x = 19.999...$

Subtracting $x$ from $10x$: $9x = 18 \Rightarrow x = 2$.

Q10

$2\sqrt{3} + \sqrt{3}$ is equal to:

(A) $2\sqrt{6}$
(B) 6
(C) $3\sqrt{3}$
(D) $4\sqrt{6}$

Correct Answer: (C) $3\sqrt{3}$

Explanation:

$2\sqrt{3} + 1\sqrt{3} = (2+1)\sqrt{3} = 3\sqrt{3}$.
