Chapter 11: Surface Areas & Volumes

Standard Level Worksheet

Back to Worksheets
  1. Question 1: A plastic box 1.5 m long, 1.25 m wide and 65 cm deep is to be made. It is opened at the top. Ignoring the thickness of the plastic sheet, determine the area of the sheet required for making the box.
    Solution:
    Step 1: Convert all dimensions to meters. Length (l) = 1.5 m, Width (b) = 1.25 m, Depth (h) = 0.65 m.
    Step 2: The box is open at the top, so the area of the sheet required is the area of the four walls plus the area of the base.
    Step 3: Area of four walls (Lateral Surface Area) = $2h(l+b) = 2 \times 0.65 \times (1.5 + 1.25) = 1.3 \times 2.75 = 3.575$ m$^2$.
    Step 4: Area of the base = $l \times b = 1.5 \times 1.25 = 1.875$ m$^2$.
    Step 5: Total area of the sheet = $3.575 + 1.875 = 5.45$ m$^2$.
  2. Question 2: The paint in a certain container is sufficient to paint an area equal to 9.375 m$^2$. How many bricks of dimensions 22.5 cm x 10 cm x 7.5 cm can be painted out of this container?
    Solution:
    Step 1: Calculate the total surface area of one brick. Dimensions in cm: l=22.5, b=10, h=7.5.
    Step 2: Total Surface Area of a brick = $2(lb + bh + hl) = 2(22.5 \times 10 + 10 \times 7.5 + 7.5 \times 22.5) = 2(225 + 75 + 168.75) = 2(468.75) = 937.5$ cm$^2$.
    Step 3: Convert the area to be painted to cm$^2$. $9.375 \text{ m}^2 = 9.375 \times 10000 \text{ cm}^2 = 93750$ cm$^2$.
    Step 4: Number of bricks = Total area to be painted / Surface area of one brick = $93750 / 937.5 = 100$ bricks.
  3. Question 3: A conical tent is 10 m high and the radius of its base is 24 m. Find the slant height of the tent and the cost of the canvas required to make the tent, if the cost of 1 m$^2$ canvas is Rs 70.
    Solution:
    Step 1: Given height (h) = 10 m and radius (r) = 24 m.
    Step 2: Slant height (l) = $\sqrt{r^2 + h^2} = \sqrt{24^2 + 10^2} = \sqrt{576 + 100} = \sqrt{676} = 26$ m.
    Step 3: The canvas required is the curved surface area of the cone. CSA = $\pi r l = \frac{22}{7} \times 24 \times 26 = \frac{13728}{7}$ m$^2$.
    Step 4: Cost of the canvas = Area $\times$ Rate = $\frac{13728}{7} \times 70 = 137280$ Rs.
  4. Question 4: A hemispherical bowl made of brass has inner diameter 10.5 cm. Find the cost of tin-plating it on the inside at the rate of Rs 16 per 100 cm$^2$.
    Solution:
    Step 1: Inner diameter = 10.5 cm, so inner radius (r) = 5.25 cm.
    Step 2: The area to be tin-plated is the inner curved surface area of the hemisphere. Inner CSA = $2\pi r^2 = 2 \times \frac{22}{7} \times (5.25)^2 = 2 \times \frac{22}{7} \times 27.5625 = 173.25$ cm$^2$.
    Step 3: The rate is Rs 16 per 100 cm$^2$. So, cost = $\frac{173.25}{100} \times 16 = 27.72$ Rs.
  5. Question 5: A right circular cylinder just encloses a sphere of radius r. Find the surface area of the sphere, curved surface area of the cylinder and the ratio of the areas obtained.
    Solution:
    Step 1: The radius of the cylinder is r, and its height is 2r.
    Step 2: Surface area of the sphere = $4\pi r^2$. Step 3: Curved surface area of the cylinder = $2\pi rh = 2\pi r(2r) = 4\pi r^2$. Step 4: The ratio of the areas = $\frac{4\pi r^2}{4\pi r^2} = \frac{1}{1}$. The ratio is 1:1.
