Chapter 8: Quadrilaterals

Standard Level Worksheet

Back to Worksheets
  1. Question 1: The angles of a quadrilateral are in the ratio 3:5:9:13. Find all the angles of the quadrilateral.
    Solution: Let the angles be $3x, 5x, 9x, 13x$. Then $3x+5x+9x+13x = 360^\circ \implies 30x = 360^\circ \implies x=12^\circ$. The angles are $36^\circ, 60^\circ, 108^\circ, 156^\circ$.
  2. Question 2: If the diagonals of a parallelogram are equal, then show that it is a rectangle.
    Solution: In $\triangle ABC$ and $\triangle DCB$ of parallelogram ABCD, $AC=DB$ (given), $AB=DC$ (opposite sides of ||gm), $BC=CB$ (common). So $\triangle ABC \cong \triangle DCB$ (SSS). Thus $\angle ABC = \angle DCB$. Since adjacent angles of a parallelogram are supplementary, $\angle ABC + \angle DCB = 180^ \implies 2\angle ABC = 180^ \implies \angle ABC = 90^$. A parallelogram with one right angle is a rectangle.
  3. Question 3: What is the Mid-point Theorem?
    Solution: The line segment joining the mid-points of two sides of a triangle is parallel to the third side and is half of it.
  4. Question 4: What is the converse of the Mid-point Theorem?
    Solution: The line drawn through the mid-point of one side of a triangle, parallel to another side, bisects the third side.
  5. Question 5: Show that the diagonals of a rhombus are perpendicular to each other.
    Solution: In a rhombus ABCD, diagonals bisect at O. In $\triangle AOB$ and $\triangle COB$, $AB=CB$ (sides of rhombus), $AO=CO$ (diagonals bisect), $BO=BO$ (common). So $\triangle AOB \cong \triangle COB$ (SSS). Thus $\angle AOB = \angle COB$. Since these form a linear pair, $\angle AOB + \angle COB = 180^ \implies 2\angle AOB = 180^ \implies \angle AOB = 90^$.
  6. Question 6: Two parallel lines l and m are intersected by a transversal p. Show that the quadrilateral formed by the bisectors of interior angles is a rectangle.
    Solution: Let the interior angles be $\angle 1, \angle 2, \angle 3, \angle 4$. The bisectors form a quadrilateral PQRS. Consecutive interior angles are supplementary, so $\angle 1 + \angle 2 = 180^$. The bisectors mean that in the triangle formed, the sum of two angles is $\frac{1}{2}(\angle 1 + \angle 2) = 90^$. So the third angle is $90^$. Similarly, all angles of PQRS are $90^$. Hence, it's a rectangle.
  7. Question 7: In a parallelogram, one angle is $80^$. Find the other three angles.
    Solution: Opposite angle is also $80^$. Adjacent angles are supplementary, so the other two angles are $180^ - 80^ = 100^$ each. Angles are $80^, 100^, 80^, 100^$.
  8. Question 8: Prove that a diagonal of a parallelogram divides it into two congruent triangles.
    Solution: Let ABCD be a parallelogram and AC be a diagonal. In $\triangle ABC$ and $\triangle CDA$, $AB || DC$ and AC is transversal, so $\angle BAC = \angle DCA$ (alternate interior angles). Similarly, $\angle BCA = \angle DAC$. $AC$ is common. By ASA congruence, $\triangle ABC \cong \triangle CDA$.
  9. Question 9: Show that if the diagonals of a quadrilateral bisect each other at right angles, then it is a rhombus.
    Solution: Let diagonals AC and BD bisect at O at $90^$. In $\triangle AOB$ and $\triangle AOD$, $BO=DO$ (given), $\angle AOB = \angle AOD = 90^$ (given), $AO$ is common. By SAS congruence, $\triangle AOB \cong \triangle AOD$. Thus $AB=AD$. Similarly, $AB=BC=CD=DA$. A quadrilateral with all sides equal is a rhombus.
  10. Question 10: ABCD is a trapezium in which $AB || DC$. E is the mid-point of AD. A line through E parallel to AB intersects BC at F. Show that F is the mid-point of BC.
    Solution: This is a direct application of the converse of the Mid-point theorem, but for a trapezium. Draw diagonal DB intersecting EF at G. In $\triangle DAB$, E is the mid-point of AD and $EG || AB$, so by converse of Mid-point theorem, G is the mid-point of DB. Now, in $\triangle BDC$, G is the mid-point of DB and $GF || DC$ (since $AB || DC$ and $EF || AB$). So by converse of Mid-point theorem, F is the mid-point of BC.
