Chapter 9: Circles

Standard Level Worksheet

Back to Worksheets
  1. Question 1: Prove that equal chords of a circle subtend equal angles at the center.
    Solution: In $\triangle AOB$ and $\triangle COD$, $OA=OC$ (radii), $OB=OD$ (radii), and $AB=CD$ (given). By SSS congruence, $\triangle AOB \cong \triangle COD$. Therefore, $\angle AOB = \angle COD$ (CPCTC).
  2. Question 2: The angle subtended by an arc at the center is double the angle subtended by it at any point on the remaining part of the circle. State True or False.
    Solution: True.
  3. Question 3: Angles in the same segment of a circle are ________.
    Solution: Equal.
  4. Question 4: What is a cyclic quadrilateral?
    Solution: A quadrilateral whose all four vertices lie on a circle.
  5. Question 5: The sum of either pair of opposite angles of a cyclic quadrilateral is ________.
    Solution: $180^\circ$.
  6. Question 6: In a circle with center O, if $\angle AOC = 130^\circ$, find $\angle ABC$.
    Solution: The angle subtended by arc AC at the center is $130^\circ$. The angle subtended at the remaining part of the circle is half of this. So, $\angle ABC = \frac{1}{2} \times \text{ reflex } \angle AOC$. Wait, it should be just $\angle AOC$. Let's re-read the theorem. "The angle subtended by an arc at the centre is double the angle subtended by it at any point on the remaining part of the circle". So $\angle AOC = 2 \angle ABC$. This means $\angle ABC = 130/2 = 65^\circ$. However, if we take the major arc, then reflex $\angle AOC = 360-130 = 230^\circ$. Then the angle would be $230/2=115^\circ$. It depends on whether A, B, C are in major or minor arc. Assuming B is in the major arc, the angle is $130/2 = 65^\circ$.
  7. Question 7: A chord of a circle is equal to its radius. Find the angle subtended by this chord at a point in the major segment.
    Solution: If chord AB = radius, then $\triangle OAB$ is equilateral. So $\angle AOB = 60^\circ$. The angle subtended at any point on the major arc is half of this, i.e., $30^\circ$.
  8. Question 8: If a line intersects two concentric circles (circles with the same center) with center O at A, B, C and D, prove that $AB = CD$.
    Solution: Draw a perpendicular OM from O to the line. For the inner circle, OM bisects BC, so $BM = MC$. For the outer circle, OM bisects AD, so $AM = MD$. Subtracting the first from the second, $AM-BM = MD-MC$, which gives $AB=CD$.
  9. Question 9: In cyclic quadrilateral ABCD, if $\angle A = 80^\circ$, find $\angle C$.
    Solution: The sum of opposite angles of a cyclic quadrilateral is $180^\circ$. So, $\angle C = 180^\circ - 80^\circ = 100^\circ$.
  10. Question 10: Prove that the perpendicular from the center of a circle to a chord bisects the chord.
    Solution: Let O be the center and AB be the chord with OM perpendicular to AB. In right triangles $\triangle OMA$ and $\triangle OMB$, $OA=OB$ (radii), $OM$ is common. By RHS congruence, $\triangle OMA \cong \triangle OMB$. Therefore, $AM=BM$ (CPCTC).
