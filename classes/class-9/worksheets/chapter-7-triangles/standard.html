Chapter 7: Triangles

Standard Level Worksheet

Back to Worksheets
  1. Question 1: In $\triangle ABC$, if $\angle A = 75^\circ$ and $\angle B = 45^\circ$, find $\angle C$.
    Solution: $\angle C = 180^\circ - (75^\circ + 45^\circ) = 180^\circ - 120^\circ = 60^\circ$.
  2. Question 2: The angles of a triangle are in the ratio 2:3:4. Find the angles.
    Solution: Let the angles be $2x, 3x, 4x$. Then $2x+3x+4x = 180^\circ \Rightarrow 9x = 180^\circ \Rightarrow x=20^\circ$. The angles are $40^\circ, 60^\circ, 80^\circ$.
  3. Question 3: In a right-angled triangle, one acute angle is $30^\circ$. Find the other acute angle.
    Solution: The other acute angle is $90^\circ - 30^\circ = 60^\circ$.
  4. Question 4: If $\triangle ABC \cong \triangle PQR$, which side of $\triangle PQR$ is equal to side $AC$?
    Solution: Side $PR$ is equal to side $AC$.
  5. Question 5: What is the AAS congruence criterion?
    Solution: Angle-Angle-Side congruence criterion states that if two angles and a non-included side of one triangle are equal to the corresponding angles and side of another triangle, then the triangles are congruent.
  6. Question 6: In $\triangle XYZ$, $XY = XZ$. If $\angle Y = 50^\circ$, find $\angle X$.
    Solution: Since $XY = XZ$, $\angle Z = \angle Y = 50^\circ$. So, $\angle X = 180^\circ - (50^\circ + 50^\circ) = 80^\circ$.
  7. Question 7: What is the RHS congruence criterion?
    Solution: Right angle-Hypotenuse-Side congruence criterion states that if the hypotenuse and one side of a right-angled triangle are equal to the hypotenuse and one side of another right-angled triangle, then the two triangles are congruent.
  8. Question 8: The sum of the lengths of any two sides of a triangle is always ________ than the third side.
    Solution: Greater.
  9. Question 9: In $\triangle PQR$, $PQ = QR$ and $\angle R = 40^\circ$. Find $\angle P$.
    Solution: Since $PQ = QR$, the angles opposite to these sides are equal. So, $\angle R = \angle P$. Given $\angle R = 40^\circ$, we have $\angle P = 40^\circ$.
  10. Question 10: An exterior angle of a triangle is $110^\circ$ and the two interior opposite angles are equal. Find the measure of each of these equal angles.
    Solution: Let each interior opposite angle be $x$. The exterior angle is the sum of the two opposite interior angles. So, $x + x = 110^\circ \Rightarrow 2x = 110^\circ \Rightarrow x = 55^\circ$.
