Chapter 7: Triangles

HOTS Level Worksheet

Back to Worksheets
  1. Question 1: In an isosceles triangle, the vertex angle is twice the sum of the base angles. Find the angles of the triangle.
    Solution: Let the base angles be $x$. Vertex angle is $2(x+x) = 4x$. Sum of angles is $x+x+4x=180^\circ \implies 6x=180^\circ \implies x=30^\circ$. Angles are $30^\circ, 30^\circ, 120^\circ$.
  2. Question 2: Prove that the sum of the three altitudes of a triangle is less than the sum of the three sides of the triangle.
    Solution: Let the altitudes from vertices A, B, C be AD, BE, CF on sides BC, CA, AB respectively. In right $\triangle ADB$, $AB > AD$. Similarly, $BC > BE$ and $CA > CF$. Adding these inequalities, we get $AB+BC+CA > AD+BE+CF$.
  3. Question 3: In a $\triangle ABC$, the bisector of $\angle B$ and $\angle C$ intersect each other at a point O. Prove that $\angle BOC = 90^\circ + \frac{1}{2}\angle A$.
    Solution: In $\triangle BOC$, $\angle BOC = 180^\circ - (\angle OBC + \angle OCB) = 180^\circ - (\frac{1}{2}\angle B + \frac{1}{2}\angle C) = 180^\circ - \frac{1}{2}(\angle B + \angle C)$. Since $\angle A+\angle B+\angle C = 180^\circ$, we have $\angle B+\angle C=180^\circ-\angle A$. So, $\angle BOC = 180^\circ - \frac{1}{2}(180^\circ-\angle A) = 180^\circ - 90^\circ + \frac{1}{2}\angle A = 90^\circ + \frac{1}{2}\angle A$.
  4. Question 4: Sides $AB$ and $AC$ of $\triangle ABC$ are extended to points $P$ and $Q$ respectively. Also, $\angle PBC < \angle QCB$. Show that $AC > AB$.
    Solution: $\angle PBC = 180^\circ - \angle ABC$. $\angle QCB = 180^\circ - \angle ACB$. Given $\angle PBC < \angle QCB$, so $180^\circ - \angle ABC < 180^\circ - \angle ACB \implies -\angle ABC < -\angle ACB \implies \angle ABC > \angle ACB$. In a triangle, the side opposite to the larger angle is longer. Thus, $AC > AB$.
  5. Question 5: $AD$ is a median of $\triangle ABC$. Is it true that $AB + BC + CA > 2AD$?
    Solution: Yes. In $\triangle ABD$, $AB+BD > AD$. In $\triangle ACD$, $AC+CD > AD$. Adding both, we get $AB+BD+AC+CD > 2AD$. Since $BD+CD=BC$, we have $AB+AC+BC > 2AD$.
  6. Question 6: Prove that the perimeter of a triangle is greater than the sum of its three medians.
    Solution: Let medians be AD, BE, CF. From the previous question, we have $AB+AC > 2AD$, $AB+BC > 2BE$, and $BC+AC > 2CF$. Adding these gives $2(AB+BC+AC) > 2(AD+BE+CF)$. Therefore, $AB+BC+AC > AD+BE+CF$.
  7. Question 7: If the altitudes from two vertices of a triangle to the opposite sides are equal, prove that the triangle is isosceles.
    Solution: Let altitudes from B and C to AC and AB be BE and CF respectively. So $BE=CF$. Consider $\triangle ABE$ and $\triangle ACF$. $\angle A$ is common. $\angle AEB = \angle AFC = 90^\circ$. By AAS congruence (using $\angle A$, one right angle, and the given equal altitudes which is a non-included side), we can't directly prove it. Let's try $\triangle BCF$ and $\triangle CBE$. Here $BC$ is common hypotenuse, $BE=CF$ is given. So by RHS congruence, $\triangle BCF \cong \triangle CBE$. This gives $\angle FBC = \angle ECB$, which means $\angle ABC = \angle ACB$. Therefore, $AC=AB$ and the triangle is isosceles.
  8. Question 8: In right triangle ABC, right-angled at C, M is the mid-point of hypotenuse AB. C is joined to M and produced to a point D such that DM = CM. Point D is joined to point B. Show that $\triangle AMC \cong \triangle BMD$.
    Solution: In $\triangle AMC$ and $\triangle BMD$, $AM=BM$ (M is mid-point), $CM=DM$ (Given), and $\angle CMA = \angle DMB$ (Vertically opposite angles). By SAS congruence, $\triangle AMC \cong \triangle BMD$.
  9. Question 9: Show that in a quadrilateral $ABCD$, $AB+BC+CD+DA > AC+BD$.
    Solution: In $\triangle ABC$, $AB+BC > AC$. In $\triangle ADC$, $AD+CD > AC$. This is for one diagonal. Now for the other diagonal BD. In $\triangle ABD$, $AB+AD > BD$. In $\triangle CBD$, $CB+CD > BD$. Let's add all four inequalities. $(AB+BC) + (AD+CD) + (AB+AD) + (CB+CD) > 2AC + 2BD$. $2AB + 2BC + 2AD + 2CD > 2AC + 2BD$. $AB+BC+CD+DA > AC+BD$. This is the correct proof.
  10. Question 10: $P$ is a point in the interior of $\triangle ABC$. Show that $AB+AC > PB+PC$.
    Solution: Extend $BP$ to intersect $AC$ at $D$. In $\triangle ABD$, $AB+AD > BD = BP+PD$. In $\triangle PDC$, $PD+DC > PC$. Adding these two inequalities, we get $AB+AD+PD+DC > BP+PD+PC$. This simplifies to $AB+(AD+DC) > BP+PC$. Since $AD+DC = AC$, we have $AB+AC > BP+PC$.
