Chapter 6: Lines and Angles

Standard Level Worksheet

Back to Worksheets
  1. Question 1: Lines AB and CD intersect at O. If $\angle AOC + \angle BOE = 70^\circ$ and $\angle BOD = 40^\circ$, find $\angle BOE$.
    Solution: $\angle AOC = \angle BOD = 40^\circ$ (Vertically opposite).
    $\angle AOC + \angle BOE = 70^\circ \Rightarrow 40^\circ + \angle BOE = 70^\circ \Rightarrow \angle BOE = 30^\circ$.
  2. Question 2: If $x + y = w + z$, prove that AOB is a line.
    Solution: Sum of angles around a point is $360^\circ$. So $(x+y) + (w+z) = 360^\circ$.
    Since $x+y = w+z$, $2(x+y) = 360^\circ \Rightarrow x+y = 180^\circ$. Thus AOB is a line.
  3. Question 3: If $PQ || ST$, $\angle PQR = 110^\circ$ and $\angle RST = 130^\circ$, find $\angle QRS$.
    Solution: Draw a line through R parallel to ST. Let angles be $x$ and $y$.
    $x + 130^\circ = 180^\circ \Rightarrow x=50^\circ$. $y + 110^\circ = 180^\circ \Rightarrow y=70^\circ$. (Wait, alternate angle is easier).
    Draw line parallel to ST through R. Angle on left = $110^\circ$ (alternate). Angle on right = $180-130=50^\circ$. $\angle QRS = 110-50 = 60^\circ$.
  4. Question 4: If two parallel lines are intersected by a transversal, prove that the bisectors of any pair of alternate interior angles are parallel.
    Solution: Let alternate angles be $\angle 1$ and $\angle 2$. $\angle 1 = \angle 2$. Bisectors make $\frac{1}{2}\angle 1$ and $\frac{1}{2}\angle 2$. Since these are equal alternate angles for the bisectors, the bisectors are parallel.
  5. Question 5: The angles of a triangle are in the ratio 2:3:4. Find the angles.
    Solution: Let angles be $2x, 3x, 4x$. Sum = $180^\circ$.
    $9x = 180^\circ \Rightarrow x = 20^\circ$. Angles are $40^\circ, 60^\circ, 80^\circ$.
  6. Question 6: Find the value of $x$ if lines $l || m$ and $x, 2x$ are interior angles on the same side of transversal.
    Solution: Consecutive interior angles are supplementary.
    $x + 2x = 180^\circ \Rightarrow 3x = 180^\circ \Rightarrow x = 60^\circ$.
  7. Question 7: If a transversal intersects two lines such that the bisectors of a pair of corresponding angles are parallel, then prove that the two lines are parallel.
    Solution: Let corresponding angles be $\angle A$ and $\angle B$. Bisectors parallel $\Rightarrow \frac{1}{2}\angle A = \frac{1}{2}\angle B$ (corresponding angles). $\Rightarrow \angle A = \angle B$. Thus lines are parallel.
  8. Question 8: In a $\triangle ABC$, if $\angle A - \angle B = 33^\circ$ and $\angle B - \angle C = 18^\circ$, find the angles.
    Solution: $\angle A = 33 + \angle B$, $\angle C = \angle B - 18$. Sum = 180.
    $(33+B) + B + (B-18) = 180 \Rightarrow 3B + 15 = 180 \Rightarrow 3B = 165 \Rightarrow B = 55^\circ$.
    $A = 88^\circ, C = 37^\circ$.
  9. Question 9: An exterior angle of a triangle is $105^\circ$ and its two interior opposite angles are equal. Find the angles.
    Solution: Ext angle = sum of int opp angles. $2x = 105^\circ \Rightarrow x = 52.5^\circ$.
    Angles are $52.5^\circ, 52.5^\circ$ and $180-105 = 75^\circ$.
  10. Question 10: Prove that the sum of the angles of a triangle is $180^\circ$.
    Solution: Draw a line through one vertex parallel to the opposite side. Use alternate interior angles property to show that the three angles form a straight angle ($180^\circ$).
