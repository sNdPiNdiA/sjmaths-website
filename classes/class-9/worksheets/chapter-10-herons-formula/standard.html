Chapter 10: Heron's Formula

Standard Level Worksheet

Back to Worksheets
  1. Question 1: An isosceles triangle has a perimeter of 30 cm and each of the equal sides is 12 cm. Find the area of the triangle.
    Solution:
    Step 1: Given perimeter = 30 cm and equal sides $a=b=12$ cm.
    Step 2: Third side $c = 30 - (12+12) = 30 - 24 = 6$ cm.
    Step 3: Calculate semi-perimeter $s = 30/2 = 15$ cm.
    Step 4: Use Heron's formula: Area = $\sqrt{15(15-12)(15-12)(15-6)} = \sqrt{15 \cdot 3 \cdot 3 \cdot 9} = \sqrt{1215} = 9\sqrt{15}$ cm$^2$.
  2. Question 2: The lengths of the sides of a triangle are 5 cm, 12 cm and 13 cm. Find the length of the perpendicular from the opposite vertex to the side whose length is 13 cm.
    Solution:
    Step 1: The sides are $a=5, b=12, c=13$. Note that $5^2+12^2 = 25+144 = 169 = 13^2$, so it is a right-angled triangle with hypotenuse 13 cm.
    Step 2: Area of the right triangle = $\frac{1}{2} \times \text{base} \times \text{height} = \frac{1}{2} \times 5 \times 12 = 30$ cm$^2$.
    Step 3: Let the perpendicular to the side of length 13 be $h$. The area can also be calculated as $\frac{1}{2} \times 13 \times h$.
    Step 4: Equate the two area calculations: $\frac{1}{2} \times 13 \times h = 30 \implies h = \frac{60}{13}$ cm.
  3. Question 3: A park, in the shape of a quadrilateral ABCD, has $\angle C = 90^\circ$, AB = 9 m, BC = 12 m, CD = 5 m and AD = 8 m. How much area does it occupy?
    Solution:
    Step 1: The quadrilateral can be split by diagonal BD into $\triangle BCD$ and $\triangle ABD$.
    Step 2: $\triangle BCD$ is a right-angled triangle at C. Area = $\frac{1}{2} \times BC \times CD = \frac{1}{2} \times 12 \times 5 = 30$ m$^2$.
    Step 3: Find the length of the diagonal BD using Pythagoras theorem in $\triangle BCD$. $BD^2 = 12^2 + 5^2 = 144+25 = 169$. So, $BD=13$ m.
    Step 4: For $\triangle ABD$, the sides are $a=9, b=8, c=13$.
    Step 5: Calculate semi-perimeter for $\triangle ABD$: $s = \frac{9+8+13}{2} = \frac{30}{2} = 15$ m.
    Step 6: Use Heron's formula for $\triangle ABD$: Area = $\sqrt{15(15-9)(15-8)(15-13)} = \sqrt{15 \cdot 6 \cdot 7 \cdot 2} = \sqrt{1260} = 6\sqrt{35} \approx 35.5$ m$^2$.
    Step 7: Total area = Area($\triangle BCD$) + Area($\triangle ABD$) = $30 + 35.5 = 65.5$ m$^2$.
  4. Question 4: Find the cost of polishing a triangular table top of sides 9, 12, 15 at the rate of Rs 5 per unit square.
    Solution:
    Step 1: Sides are $a=9, b=12, c=15$. Notice $9^2+12^2=81+144=225=15^2$. It's a right triangle.
    Step 2: Area = $\frac{1}{2} \times 9 \times 12 = 54$ square units.
    Step 3: Cost = Area $\times$ Rate = $54 \times 5 = 270$ Rs.
  5. Question 5: The perimeter of an isosceles triangle is 32 cm. The ratio of the equal side to its base is 3:2. Find the area of the triangle.
    Solution:
    Step 1: Let the equal sides be $3x$ and the base be $2x$. Step 2: Perimeter = $3x+3x+2x = 8x = 32 \implies x=4$. Step 3: The sides are $a=12, b=12, c=8$. Step 4: Semi-perimeter $s=32/2=16$. Step 5: Area = $\sqrt{16(16-12)(16-12)(16-8)} = \sqrt{16 \cdot 4 \cdot 4 \cdot 8} = \sqrt{2048} = 32\sqrt{2}$ cm$^2$.
  6. Question 6: A rhombus shaped field has green grass for 18 cows to graze. If each side of the rhombus is 30 m and its longer diagonal is 48 m, how much area of grass field will each cow be getting?
    Solution:
    Step 1: A rhombus is made of two congruent triangles. Let the rhombus be ABCD with side 30m and diagonal AC=48m.
    Step 2: Consider $\triangle ABC$. Sides are $a=30, b=30, c=48$. Step 3: Semi-perimeter $s = (30+30+48)/2 = 108/2 = 54$ m. Step 4: Area of $\triangle ABC = \sqrt{54(54-30)(54-30)(54-48)} = \sqrt{54 \cdot 24 \cdot 24 \cdot 6} = 432$ m$^2$. Step 5: Total area of rhombus = $2 \times 432 = 864$ m$^2$. Step 6: Area per cow = Total Area / Number of cows = $864 / 18 = 48$ m$^2$.
  7. Question 7: Two sides of a triangular field are 85 m and 154 m in length and its perimeter is 324 m. Find the area of the field.
    Solution:
    Step 1: Perimeter = 324 m. Sides are $a=85, b=154$. Step 2: Third side $c = 324 - (85+154) = 324 - 239 = 85$ m. Oh, it's an isosceles triangle.
    Step 3: Semi-perimeter $s=324/2=162$ m. Step 4: Area = $\sqrt{162(162-85)(162-85)(162-154)} = \sqrt{162 \cdot 77 \cdot 77 \cdot 8} = 77 \sqrt{162 \cdot 8} = 77\sqrt{1296} = 77 \times 36 = 2772$ m$^2$.
  8. Question 8: Find the percentage increase in the area of a triangle if its each side is doubled.
    Solution:
    Step 1: Original Area = $\sqrt{s(s-a)(s-b)(s-c)}$.
    Step 2: New sides are $2a, 2b, 2c$. New semi-perimeter $s' = \frac{2a+2b+2c}{2} = 2s$. Step 3: New Area = $\sqrt{s'(s'-2a)(s'-2b)(s'-2c)} = \sqrt{2s(2s-2a)(2s-2b)(2s-2c)} = \sqrt{16s(s-a)(s-b)(s-c)} = 4 \times \text{Original Area}$.
    Step 4: Increase in area = New Area - Original Area = 3 x Original Area.
    Step 5: Percentage increase = $\frac{\text{Increase}}{\text{Original Area}} \times 100 = \frac{3 \times \text{Original Area}}{\text{Original Area}} \times 100 = 300\%$.
